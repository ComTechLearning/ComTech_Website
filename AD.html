ASSEMBLE/DISASSEMBLE of System Unit

Lesson 3

Disassemble of System Unit

Step 1: Check if the computer is functioning by turning it on.

Step 2:Power down the computer and unplug the power supply from the wall and the system.

Step 3;Disconnect any external hardware or devices connected to the front panel.

Step 4:Remove the side panel on the right-hand side of the computer case.

Step 5:Detach the 4-pin, SATA data, USB F.P., audio F.P., and front panel connectors from Step 6:the motherboard and power supply.

Step 7:Unscrew and remove the power supply unit (PSU) along with its attached cables.

Step 8:Disconnect and remove the hard disk drive (HDD) from its bracket or cage.

Step 9:Release the clips and carefully remove the memory module (RAM).

Step 10:Unscrew and remove the motherboard from the case, ensuring all connections are detached.

Assemmble of System Unit

Step 1: Attach Motherboard and position it evenly

step 2: Make sure the screw holes are aligned properly

step 3: Insert Screws to the corresponding holes

step 4: Attach the HDD then insert screws properly

step 5: Attach the Power Supply then screw tightly

step 6: Attach the RAM in the RAM slot

step 7: Attach the 24-Pin Power Connector to its assigned port

(make sure to properly align it before attaching)

step 8: Attach the 4-Pin connector to its assigned port

step 9: Carefully attach the Front-Panel Connectors according to their assigned locations

(make sure they are attached firmly in place)

step 10: Attach the Audio Front Panel Connector

step 11: Attach USB Front Panel Connector

Step 12: Attach SATA Data Connector to the corresponding slot

step 13: Make sure every connection is stable and secure

step 14: Close the side panel and lock in place

step 15: Connect the power, HDMI, and other attachments necessary for the computer

step 16: Turn the PC on to see if it is functional
